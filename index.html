FDS Folder Guide

What Each FDS Folder Is For

Use this page when you know the kind of question you need answered, but not which folder to open first.

ChartField Statements

Detailed or summary views of financial activity by department, project, initiative, segment, site, or other ChartString values.

Answers: What is my available budget balance for the current fiscal year?

Claim on Cash Reports

Cash balance and liquidity reporting for departments or funds, including whether cash is available to support activity.

Answers: Does my department have actual liquid cash to cover expenses?

Consolidated Operating Reports (COB)

High-level budget management reports comparing budget versus actuals against the annual operating plan.

Answers: Are we over or under our planned annual budget?

Endowment and Gift Reports

Restricted fund reporting for gifts and endowments, including market value, spending distributions, and available balances.

Answers: How much can I spend from this donor-restricted gift or endowment?

Financial Statements

Formal unit-level reports showing overall financial position, including assets, liabilities, net assets, and activity.

Answers: What is the overall financial health of my unit?

Fiscal Year-End Reports

Close-period reports used in June and July to confirm transactions, accruals, P-Cards, vouchers, POs, and year-end cleanup.

Answers: What is left to close the books correctly?

Fringe Benefit Reports

Benefit-related costs charged to departments or grants, including fringe detail, summary, and trends.

Answers: Why are personnel costs higher than base salaries?

Fund Balance / Overdraft Reports

Bottom-line health reporting that identifies accounts in deficit or with available carry-forward balances.

Answers: Which accounts are in the red and need attention?

Payroll Reports

Salary and employee-level payroll reporting, including who was paid, how much, when, and from which funding source.

Answers: Is the right person being paid the right amount from the right account?

Procurement Reports

Purchasing activity reports covering POs, open commitments, P-Card activity, SAPOs, vouchers, and vendor payments.

Answers: Which vendors were paid, and what POs remain open?

Project Lifecycle Reports

Grant, sponsored, capital, and project reports that track activity across the full project life, not just one fiscal year.

Answers: What is total spending since the project started?

Trial Balance Reports

Accounting-focused reports used to verify balances and investigate the specific journal or transaction behind a change.

Answers: What transaction or journal entry caused this total to change?
Golden Rule: If the question is “How much?”, start with a Summary report. If the question is “Why?”, go to a Detail report.
